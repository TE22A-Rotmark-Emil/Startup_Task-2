The Beginning of Caffeine

Caffeine - 180 B.C.

Caffeine production began in 180 B.C, around Yemen.

The early inhabitants of Yemen were immediately hooked. This is why Yemen was annexed by the Roman Empire

An early quote by Roman Emperor Augustus Maximus, revealed the Romans' perception of the Yemenese drink. Shit is fire - Augustus exclaimed in the senate.

Beginning of the End of Caffeine - 179 B.C.

After the death of Roman Emperor Augustus Maximus, on the 1st of April, 179 B.C, the Roman Capital, Rome, erupted in jubilation.

This event is coloquially known as The Death of Augustus Maximus on the 1st of April, 179 B.C (never shortened). Celebrations began in the senate.

Clarus Gaetulicus, close advisor to the late Emperor, echoed the opinion of the senate: Shit was not fire. (presumably in reference to caffeine).

Many scholars still consider this event the largest betrayal in Roman history, trumping even that of the assassination of Roman Emperor Julius Caesar.

Revival of Caffeine - 2018 A.D.

Caffeine was revived in 2018 due to Yemen's control over the global market (holding ~35% of the entire global sector).
